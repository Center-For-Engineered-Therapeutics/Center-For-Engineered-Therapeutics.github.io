POSITIONS OPEN

Position Title: T32 Postdoctoral Trainee – Translational Orthopaedic Research Training Program (Topic: Bone metastasis and/or bone infection)

Center for Engineered Therapeutics at the Department of Medicine, Brigham and Women’s Hospital, and Harvard Medical School is seeking an excellent Animal Technician for conducting in vivo studies with the laboratory researchers and assisting basic laboratory management with the PIs.

Location: Boston, Massachusetts, USA (Brigham and Women’s Hospital, Harvard Medical School)

Schedule: Full-time, 24-36 months. This position will start before July 2023.

We are seeking a highly motivated and talented postdoctoral trainee who is highly committed to developing a successful research career with a focus area of research on bone metastasis and/or bone infection. This T32 mentored postdoctoral training program is offered at the Department of Orthopaedic Surgery, Brigham and Women’s Hospital. The postdoctoral trainee will be mentored by the Co-Directors and faculty members at the Center for Engineered Therapeutics, which is located at the interface of the Department of Orthopaedic Surgery and Department of Medicine, within the rich ecosystem of Brigham and Women’s Hospital, Harvard Medical School, and the Harvard-MIT Division of Health Sciences and Technology (HST). The Center for Engineered Therapeutics provides an interdisciplinary research environment where biomedical engineers, biologists, chemists, pharmacists, material scientists, and clinicians work together to solve biological challenges with holistic and creative approaches. We develop novel biotechnologies for clinical translation based on the understanding of biological mechanisms. We love addressing exciting and hard problems that will have a high impact on patients’ lives. Please check our publications (examples of our recent research articles include https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-021-01000-4 and https://www.nature.com/articles/s41551-022-00950-x.)

The center will provide training in experiment design, result analysis, scientific writing, and other guidance for the career development. We are fully equipped for material synthesis and characterization, bioengineering, immunochemistry, synthetic chemistry, pharmacology, molecular biology, in vitro assays and in vivo studies. In addition to professional research training in the center, the researchers will be exposed to high-level seminars and conferences held at Harvard and MIT.

Minimum Education and Experience Requirements: Postdoctoral trainee candidate will have completed highly relevant doctoral training in biomedical sciences, chemistry, pharmacy, or material science and engineering related area and awarded a Ph.D., M.D., M.D./Ph.D., or equivalent prior to starting the program. Candidates who already have post-doctoral training experience are also eligible.

Required Qualifications, Competencies, and Experience: All applicants must be either US citizens, non-citizen nationals or permanent residents, by the time they will begin this program. The candidates should have less than one year of T32 or F support in the past.

Interested applicants should send a curriculum vitae to Dr. Shiladitya Sengupta (ssengupta2@bwh.harvard.edu) and Dr. Hae Lin Jang (hjang@bwh.harvard.edu) by Feb, 2023.

Additional information: Preferred candidates will have strong publication record.
